Hackly

  • Discord

  • A wiki-style Hackathon

  • A 3-day online event to launch open source project through online discussions.
  • Join the discord group

Alongside the desire to give back to the community, most developers contribute either to improve tools they (could) use or out of intellectual curiosity. But, contributing to open source is time-consuming and requires a good understanding of codebases and guidelines.

Meanwhile, promising open-source projects often fail to take off because creators carry the entire weight before the projects are usable, let alone attract contributors. Missing the collective intelligence needed to grasp all opportunities and risk to deliver value at the end.

Gathering a community at the start of a project could solve both these problems, by sharing the workload and making the right fundamental choices at the beginning of any valuable project. Hackly is a three-day sprint to test this approach.

at a glance

Step one : Share a project ideas. The aim will be to work in a top-down manner. If you have ideas, you can already share it in your discord.

Step two : Vote for the projects you’d like to work on, and talk about it with other people interessted.

Step three (begins december 27) : Break down the idea and see how it could be improved and the first version of the idea can be launched, with an emphasis on extensibility and versioning to support future development.

Online! We’ll use Discord for discussion and Github for hosting.

There is no theme. The goal is to let participants free to work on projects they found valuable, without focusing on commercial value or project difficulty.

All solutions will be open source and freely accessible. This transparency ensures that everyone can benefit from and build upon the shared knowledge.
