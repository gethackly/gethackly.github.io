Hackly

PLEASE NOTE: Hackly will only be open to registered participants.

  • No teams, no judges, no theme.

  • Hackly is an online ideathon – a group brainstorming event for new ideas with rewards but no restrictions.

Hackly is a 5-day event where participants worldwide brainstorm to solve problems together. From defining the problem to designing solutions, everything is shaped by the community, allowing everyone to share, improve, and build on each other's ideas. Top contributors earn rewards.

This wiki-style approach connects you to a broader network, eliminating the need to find teammates. You can contribute at your own pace and focus on what you want, whenever you want, while leveraging collective intelligence to inspire breakthrough ideas.

Our Ideathon at a glance


Much like how Wikipedia gathers encyclopedic articles or open-source projects to manage code files, this event will centralize community ideas in an article that explains solutions to specific problems. This document will be accessible to all, and the aim is to create collaborative content as the community discusses it.

Days 1-2: Sharing Problems
Participants will share the problems they want to address, serving as the introduction to the article. The community will refine these contributions to clarify the issues, with no thematic restrictions or commercial value constraints.

Days 3-5: Idea Proposals
To build the main body of the article, participants will propose ideas for addressing the identified problems. The community will discuss potential risks and challenges, working together to find ways to overcome these obstacles.

We believe that the most impactful contributions are those that generate discussion and foster collaborative development. To encourage this, we rank contributors according to the interactions generated by their contributions. Reward money will be distributed to the top 10 contributors, rather than to individual projects.

We're aiming to hold the event from December 18 to 23, with participation limited to the first 300 sign-ups. By joining, you’ll help us attract sponsors and enhance the experience. It’s free, and we’ll only reach out with relevant updates and opportunities—no ads or spam.

There are a multitude of ways to contribute, from asking questions, to documenting arguments, to picking up on other ideas, to criticizing, to creating new proposals... Even incomplete contributions are valued, as they can stimulate discussion and enrich collective thinking.

All solutions will be open source and freely accessible. This transparency ensures that everyone can benefit from and build upon the shared knowledge.
