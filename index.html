Our hackathon is massively collaborative — no teams, just a place where everyone can share ideas and build on each other. So you can contribute as much or as little as you like and skip the search for teammates.

We'll use Github for hosting and discussions will take place in a dedicated subreddit. We consider a valuable contribution to be one that sparks debate within the group.

With this in mind, we developed a Reddit bot that ranks participant's influence within the community, with scores displayed on a leaderboard.

Our Ideathon at a glance

An event during which people come together for several days to work on a collaborative computer programming or digital creation project. Traditionally closed and carried out by small teams, we have rethought this format to make it easier to access and capitalize on the collective intelligence of the whole group.

We're looking for sponsors and hope to launch the first event at the end of December. By registering you increase the value of our event and speed up the process. Also registration will close before the event starts. We’ll only send essential updates—no spam or promos.

There are a multitude of ways to contribute, from asking questions, to documenting arguments, to picking up on other ideas, to criticizing, to creating new proposals... Even incomplete contributions are valued, as they can stimulate discussion and enrich collective thinking.

There are no judges, so there are no winning solutions and no prize. The goal is to reflect on projects for the common good, without focusing on commercial value or project difficulty.

Also, the jury's subjectivity can foster an overly competitive atmosphere, resulting in superficial solutions aimed merely at meeting the winning criteria. Here, the focus is on fostering team spirit.

All solutions will be open source and freely accessible. This transparency ensures that everyone can benefit from and build upon the shared knowledge.
